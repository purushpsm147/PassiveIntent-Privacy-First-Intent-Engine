Detect hesitation while the session is still alive.

PassiveIntent is a privacy-first JavaScript library for product, growth, and CX teams that want to catch checkout friction, pricing doubt, and exit intent before the user disappears, without exporting behavioral data to another vendor.

What it is

A small client-side library for teams that need to act before abandonment.

Use it in checkout, pricing, billing, onboarding, and support-sensitive journeys where hesitation matters more than hindsight.

The shipping product is PassiveIntent Core. JavaScript and React packages are available now.

Why PassiveIntent

Built for intervention logic, not surveillance plumbing.

Most teams discover friction after the drop-off. PassiveIntent keeps the model close to the session so you can respond while the decision is still in motion.

It is lightweight enough to ship broadly, private enough for sensitive flows, and specific enough to support real product decisions instead of another dashboard.

Download the Zero Egress Attestation (PDF)

Privacy-first

No behavioral export by default

Intent evaluation stays close to the session instead of feeding a remote clickstream.

Zero-egress

No external inference vendor required

You can detect hesitation without adding another behavioral processor to your stack.

Lightweight

Small enough to ship broadly

At roughly 11 kB gzip, the library fits storefronts, onboarding flows, and SaaS apps.

No Black Box

Not AI. Deterministic math.

No black-box AI. Just provable, deterministic session math built from Markov chains, entropy, and dwell-time signals your team can inspect and trust.

How it works

A small client-side loop: observe, infer, intervene.

Route changes and lifecycle events feed the model. The model scores hesitation locally. Your product decides how to respond.

Intervene

Support, reassurance, offers, or the next best step.

FAQ

What teams usually need answered before they adopt.

What is the main job PassiveIntent solves?

Recovering users in the last moments before abandonment while intent is still active.

Do we need to send behavioral data to your servers?

No. Intent evaluation runs locally in the browser by default.

Do we need a cookie banner for PassiveIntent to work?

No. PassiveIntent itself does not require analytics cookies or server-side behavioral tracking.

Is this only for engineers?

No. Product, growth, and CX teams can use the signals to drive practical intervention playbooks.

Can I use standard tiers to wrap PassiveIntent into my own SDK or app?

No. Standard commercial tiers are for internal business use. Agency, OEM, or wrapped-product scenarios require a separate license.
